Meet Sharon

Sharon is passionate about student wellbeing, liberation, and creating a university environment where every student feels supported, heard, and empowered.

She has worked to improve mental health systems, student safety, disability inclusion, and opportunities for student success. Her focus is on building a student-centred system that works for everyone.

Achievements

What Sharon Has Already Achieved

Real results that have made a difference for students across Manchester.

Mental Health
🧠

Mental Health Self-Referral

Sharon advocated for student-centred procedures and worked with the University of Manchester Mental Health Support Team to develop a self-referral form, making it easier for students to access their first appointment.

ADHD Support
💡

ADHD Provision at City Level

As GMSP Health Officer, Sharon worked to improve ADHD provision at city and NHS level and held the first stakeholder meeting to improve services for students with ADHD.

Campus Safety
🌙

Reclaim the Night

Sharon advocated for safer streets and campuses through improved lighting, better reporting systems, and accountability for venues — championing students' right to feel safe.

Ongoing Work

Manchester Graduate Talent Program

Working on expanding the Manchester Graduate Talent Program to improve employment opportunities and career pathways for students post-graduation.

Disability

Disability Allyship Program

Manifesto 2026 - 27

This Year's Manifesto

A clear vision for what Sharon will fight for in 2026 - 27.

01
💚

Student-Centred Mental Health

Mental health support should be clear, accessible, and designed around students' needs. Sharon will reshape the mental health system so students can easily navigate support without confusion.

  • Support for disabled students
  • Inclusion for international students
  • Uplift minoritised students
  • No student faces struggles alone
02
🎓

Support for PGT Students

PGT students often feel invisible in student support systems. Sharon will improve representation and ensure wellbeing services are designed specifically for postgraduate students.

  • Dedicated PGT wellbeing pathways
  • Improved representation
  • Tailored mental health services
  • Stronger postgrad community
03
🌃

Safer Campus

Sharon will continue the Reclaim the Night campaign to build a university campus and city where every student feels safe walking home — at any hour.

  • Better lighting on campus
  • Transparent reporting systems
  • Enhanced safety measures
  • Consent education programs
